A Direct Approach to Hardware Returns

Our end-to-end process delivers seamless hardware replacements with maximum transparency and minimal customer effort.

Our Vision Statement

This process is designed to provide a seamless, low-effort customer experience that minimizes downtime while ensuring we receive valuable hardware for engineering analysis. The process prioritizes clear communication, speed, and logistical simplicity for the customer, turning a negative product experience into a positive support interaction.

Positive Impact by Function

Customer Experience (CX)

Current State

Inconsistent and stressful. Customers face uncertainty, variable wait times, and have to manage complex shipping for large items themselves. This erodes trust in our new brand.

Future State

Predictable and premium. Clear, consistent communication and white-glove service create a positive, loyalty-building experience.

Product & Engineering

Current State

A critical missed opportunity. Defective units arrive poorly packaged (if at all), without a consistent next step. This prevents our hardware teams from doing proper root cause analysis, so we can't learn from failures.

Future State

A rich data source for improvement. The process is designed to get units back in debuggable condition. JIRA tracking ensures every failure is analyzed, feeding a direct loop of data to engineering to build more reliable products and reduce the rate of RMA.

Finance

Current State

Financial Risk = High. We ship replacements with no formal process to ensure the defective unit is returned. Every non-return is a 100% loss of a valuable asset.

Future State

Controlled and predictable. A formalized process protects against asset loss. Costs are predictable and can be budgeted as part of the cost of goods sold (COGS).

Customer Success & Operations

Current State

High manual overhead. Internal teams spend excessive time tracking shipments, updating stakeholders, and coordinating logistics reactively. It's not scalable and is prone to human error.

Future State

Scalable and automated. A defined JIRA workflow, templated communications, and clear vendor processes reduce the manual workload our team, freeing them up to help more customers. The process runs itself.

Supply Chain & Logistics

Current State

Chaotic. No clear visibility into how many units are out for RMA, their status, or what is available for internal use. Risk of stockouts for new sales due to unplanned RMA shipments.

Future State

Clear and organized. The link between the JIRA ticket and the new sales order provides full visibility. Salvageable units are tracked and provisioned for internal teams, supporting demand.

Open Questions

"No-Return" Policy

What happens if a customer doesn't return the defective unit within a specified timeframe (i.e. 30 days)?

  • The industry standard is to secure a credit card hold for the full value of the replacement unit, which is released upon return.
  • Define processes for "never ships" vs. "logistics failure" (e.g., carrier loses package).

Customer Communication Templates

Draft the email templates for each stage of the process (RMA approved, replacement shipped, return instructions, return received, return reminder).

The "Return Kit"

How do we get necessary labels in the box?

Vendor Agreements

Solidify the process with them. How do they receive pickup requests? What information do they provide back to you and how quickly? What are the SLAs?

Internal Training

Document this SOP and train the team on the exact steps, especially on the importance of capturing serial numbers and linking the JIRA ticket to the sales order.

Metrics for Success (KPIs)

Implement ways to measure:

  • Time-to-Resolution (TTR): From initial ticket to replacement shipment.
  • Return Success Rate: Percentage of defective units successfully returned.
  • Asset Condition Score: A simple 1-5 rating by the receiving team on the condition of the returned unit.
  • Customer Satisfaction (CSAT): Send a survey after the process is complete.

Customer Return Process

Customer Responsibility

Repackaging the card in the anti-static bag and custom box insert the new card arrived in.

Return Logistics

  • A pre-paid, pre-addressed return shipping label (e.g., FedEx or UPS) is included in the "Return Kit."
  • Customer seals the box, applies the new label over the old one, and drops it off at a designated carrier location.

JIRA Workflow

The tracking number on the provided label is key.

Customer Responsibility

Minimal. The customer needs to ensure the system is safely powered down, disconnected, and ready for repackaging.

Return Logistics: White Glove Pickup

When delivering the replacement unit, the vendor will repackage the defective unit, apply all necessary labels, and take possession of the system. This removes the burden of shipping from the customer.

Vendor Management

Requires a clear process with Vendor to ensure they provide tracking and reference numbers back to us promptly.

Customer Responsibility

Minimal. The customer needs to ensure the system is safely powered down, disconnected, and removed from the rack.

Return Logistics: White Glove Pickup (Enhanced)

  • This is identical to the Desktop System process but may require more coordination.
  • Vendors should be prepared for heavier, bulkier items and may need to bring specialized equipment (pallet jack, etc.) depending on the customer's facility (e.g., no loading dock).
  • The cost is higher, but the guaranteed safe return of a high-value enterprise asset is paramount.
